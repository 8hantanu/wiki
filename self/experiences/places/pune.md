# pune


### Day 1

#### 09:00

Reached early morning.

#### 13:30

Left for car rental. Shitty experience with Zoomcar—not unlocking. Finally bargained for cheap and got a good SUV for just ₹1700.

Shivam drove for the first half-day, headed to Bhimashankar Jyotirlinga.

#### 17:30

At around 17:30 took a snacks break—had dosa. The road was pretty, with wet roads and scenic views. We stopped at a gorge for a quick photo break. We resumed the drive now ETA at 2000. It was getting dark and visibility was low with heavy rains.

### Day 1 (continued – Bhimashankar)

#### 20:30

Reached the temple premises around 20:30. It was raining heavily and we had to walk around half a kilometre to reach the main temple. It was a fun walk. We hadn’t figured out a place to stay yet. On talking to the priest, we came to know about a small lodge beside the temple. It’s been a ritual of ours for a long time that we close down temples—this time was the same too.

#### 22:30

For dinner we had piping hot Maggi. Then we walked back to get the car to the lodge. The road was quite narrow there. We dozed of.

#### Morning

Only to be woken four hours later, struggling to time-share a common washroom.
We did our darshan/archana and headed back to Pune. On the way we went to meet an aunt, had a hearty meal, and spent the afternoon in siesta. The next day, Sunday, we headed out again.

---

## Pune & Nearby Exploration

### Sunday

#### Morning

Headed to Visapur with a rental scooty. The rental scooty key broke, then got another one and headed to Visapur. The view again was scenic. The trek was exhilarating, unlike before. 

We climbed through a waterfall incarnated from the heavy rain flow, cutting its way through the mountain. The trek wasn’t long—just about 4 km—but the last 500 m were tough, literally climbing up rocks, trying to find footing and getting down equally carefully.

#### Evening

Came back to Pune to see off our parent. Returned the scooty to the rental place. A lazy day, then dinner at Lane 6.

#### Next Day

Early morning we headed to Baner hill. It was a pretty trail amidst the city, many paths to explore. Came back and then slept.
